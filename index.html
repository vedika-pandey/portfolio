How I work

From ambiguity to audit-ready output — every engagement follows the same underlying logic, regardless of the starting point.

01 — Diagnose

Understand before designing

Map the data landscape, regulatory obligations, and the gap between current state and required output. Produce a compliance readiness picture before touching any framework.

Outputs
Gap analysis · compliance heatmap · data audit · ESRS readiness mapping
02 — Architect

Design the system, not just the report

Design the KPI framework, methodology, governance, and data flows — before collection begins. The architecture determines whether the outputs survive scrutiny.

Outputs
KPI architecture · GHG methodology · DMA design · data governance model
03 — Deliver

Execute under real constraints

Run the data collection, analysis, stakeholder coordination, and reporting — under the actual data conditions that exist, not the ideal conditions assumed by the framework.

Outputs
Emissions model · supplier coordination · controversy screening · disclosure draft
04 — Govern

Build for independence, not dependency

Build the systems, documentation, and processes that make the work repeatable and organisationally owned after I leave. Dependency is a failure mode.

Outputs
Audit trail · evidence management · process documentation · assurance-ready packaging
Design for scrutiny

Every framework, model, and process is built to survive assurance — not optimised for first-year completion. Year-two problems are expensive.

Constrained realism

I design methodologies that work with imperfect data — because that is the only data that exists in practice. Perfect is the enemy of defensible.

Organisational handover

The goal is always a system the organisation can operate independently. I document everything and transfer ownership as a core deliverable, not an afterthought.

Building ESG infrastructure for first-time CSRD compliance across global operations

The situation

A global maritime operations group preparing for first-time CSRD disclosure. No existing ESG data infrastructure, a Scope 3 footprint distributed across a 50+ supplier network, and a regulatory deadline with external assurance implications. Operations spanning multiple geographies with no unified sustainability data system and no prior reporting framework experience.

The core problem

Most CSRD compliance failures happen not because organisations lack sustainability intent — but because their data infrastructure wasn't designed for audit. The challenge was building a system that could produce defensible outputs in year one and scale reliably in year two, without creating a disclosure programme the organisation couldn't sustain.

Strategic judgment
"Designed for assurance from day one — not optimised for first-year completion. A DMA that overscopes creates a disclosure programme no organisation can sustain. Scope was deliberately calibrated to what was strategically significant and what the data could actually support."
Four-phase delivery
01
Diagnostic & gap analysis
Data landscape audit, existing policy review, ESRS requirements mapped to current state, compliance readiness heatmap produced per ESRS standard.
→ Gap analysis report
02
Double materiality assessment
Stakeholder mapping, dual impact and financial materiality analysis across the value chain, 15+ material ESG topics prioritised and mapped to ESRS topical standards.
→ DMA report + ESRS disclosure scope
03
Data architecture & GHG accounting
80+ KPI framework designed across E/S/G dimensions. Scope 1–3 hybrid methodology built — activity-based where data existed, spend-based estimation with documented assumptions for long-tail suppliers. 50+ supplier data collection coordinated.
→ KPI architecture + Scope 1–3 emissions model
04
Governance & disclosure
Version-controlled evidence files for every data point, audit trail documentation, annual process design for ongoing operation. Investor-facing CSRD/ESRS-aligned report produced.
→ CSRD disclosure + governance system

Context

Embedded with Amundi Asset Management's ESG research team in Paris. Analysed ESG disclosures of healthcare companies across Europe, Asia, and North America — covering social, human rights, and governance factors — to inform investment assessments. Screened controversies across 300+ issuers, prioritising labour and human rights issues for portfolio oversight. Facilitated stewardship engagements on ESG performance and governance accountability.

What made it different

Buy-side ESG analysis requires a fundamentally different calibration than consulting work. Outputs must be decision-useful to portfolio managers within tight timelines, credible to compliance and stewardship functions, and defensible under external scrutiny. The discipline of producing sector briefings that are concise but complete — and that translate ESG findings into the language of investment risk — is a different skill from building implementation frameworks.

Working principle
"Buy-side ESG is not about frameworks — it's about materiality to investment thesis. I learned to translate ESG findings into the language of risk, exposure, and portfolio implication that investment teams actually respond to."

Case study 03 · Value chain risk · PE advisory

Supply chain ESG risk and PE portfolio assessment across agri-food and FMCG

Nov 2023 – present · Argo Advisory
Context

Senior sustainability consultant at Argo Advisory, working with agri-food and FMCG clients to identify and manage material ESG risks across supply chains and business operations. Simultaneously supported PE firms in evaluating portfolio ESG performance — with findings feeding directly into investment decisions and fund-level due diligence processes.

The work

ESG risk assessments applying CSRD, TNFD, and CSDDD frameworks across supply chains. Translated findings into strategic recommendations for client leadership, informing sustainability strategy and operational decision-making. For PE clients: portfolio-level ESG performance evaluation with outputs framed specifically for investment committee and fund-level reporting requirements.

What made it complex

Agri-food supply chains are where ESG risk is most concentrated and least visible. Deforestation, labour rights, water use, and biodiversity exposure are all material but difficult to quantify without primary supplier data. The work required consistent judgment about what level of analysis was decision-useful versus methodologically elegant — and how to communicate complex supply chain risk to leadership audiences who needed to act on it.

Working principle
"ESG findings that don't connect to investment thesis don't get used. The translation layer — from supply chain risk to portfolio implication — is where the value sits."
